Project Name Here

Fantasy Football users rejoice! Welcome to the hottest new way to do FFL. This project will be a fantasy football (NFL) single page app (SPA). Fantasy football is centered around creating a “fantasy” team out of real life NFL players, and pitting your fantasy team against other teams. Groups of users, usually 6-8, will create their own teams as part of a “league”, and will compete against other individuals within that league.

Individuals in a fantasy football league will choose real players in the American National Football League (NFL) for their teams, and these teams will face each other weekly in a one-on-one (aka head-to-head) matchup during actual NFL games. Players in the real games perform actions that score points in fantasy football, and whichever fantasy team scores more points in that matchup wins. Teams with the best win/loss records enter fantasy playoffs, and an eventual champion is selected.

Makeup of a fantasy team

The main (splash) page will consist of live NFL fantasy football league news and information using an API call. This information will be scrolling across the page like a carousel or a ticker. The app will have a navigation bar. In the nav bar, it will allow users to sign up and/or login to the site and allow users to navigate the page. It may use breadcrumbs as well to track user progress through the SPA. There will be a footer at the bottom of the SPA with copyright information. There will be a current roster of all 32 NFL teams and a list of the highest ranking fantasy players according to last years stats. Initially a user may make his own league and become a commissioner. The league consists of a max of 8 teams (Minimum of six). The league has a name chosen by the commissioner and the commissioner and only the commissioner can give points to each team's starting roster. The commissioner can own a team and is the only user that can add or delete a team.

There are 32 NFL teams, and your fantasy team will consist of players from some of these teams.

There are lots of different positions on a NFL team, but for fantasy football purposes, we simplify the different positions greatly: all you have to worry about is Quarterback (QB), Running back (RB), Wide Receiver (WR), Tight End (TE), Kicker (K), and Defense/Special Teams (D/ST). Defense/Special Teams is a special position on your roster, it represents the entire Defense and Special Teams units, which are made up of many players, for one of the 32 teams. All other players you select will be individual players.

How players score points isn’t important right now, you can worry about that later. For those of you familiar with fantasy football, this application will operate under standard scoring rules.

Fantasy teams will have 16 members, but only 9 of them will actually count towards scoring in the weekly matchup against another fantasy team. The other 7 will remain on the team’s “bench”, and any points they score will not count towards your team’s total that week. Your team, and every team in the league, will select players in a “fantasy draft” – more about that later.

Our fantasy team’s 9-player active roster will have 1 Quarterback (QB), 2 Running backs (RB), 2 Wide Receivers (WR), 1 Tight End (TE), 1 Flex (which can either be a RB, WR, or TE), 1 Kicker (K), and 1 Defense/Special Teams (D/ST).
